A place where female entrepreneurs can trust and fall back on throughout their endeavors.

Today, women-owned businesses represent 42 percent of the 13 million businesses in the U.S. There is a real need for mentoring programs and resources to assist a new generation of entrepreneurs.

Start Here

The U.S. Small Business Administration

Women’s Business Center has 122 locations across the country that offer programs and seminars in everything from one-on-one counseling to financing resources.

Minority Business Development Agency

According to a study from Forbes, minority-owned business owners “encounter higher borrowing costs, receive smaller loans and see their loan applications rejected more often than others. Yet, women of color account for 89% of new businesses started every day in the past year! As an agency with the Department of Congress, MBDA works with minority-owned business owners to help them reach new markets, especially through funding and scaling.

37 Angels

This community of women angel investors offer more than just money. The organization also connects women entrepreneurs with seasoned mentors to help them with issues in everything from funding to marketing. Turning to an organization like 37 Angels can also help women entrepreneurs widen their professional network.

Small Business Development Centers (SBDC)

Small Business Development Centers provide counseling and training to small businesses including working with the SBA to develop and provide informational tools to support business start-ups and existing business expansion.
