Funding & Grants

Grants

Monthly Amber Grant, $10,000
Five applicants are chosen monthly for this grant. One is the Amber Grant winner who will receive $10,000. The four remaining applicants will receive $1,000 each. The Amber Grant winners will qualify for a grant of $25,000 awarded at the end of the year."

Annual Amber Grant, $25,000
This grant is awarded to one of the 12 winners of the Monthly Amber Grant.

The Marketing Grant, Marketing Help
This grant is given to three people every year and offers marketing assistance from experts at WomensNet

The Non-Profit Grant, 5,000
Every quarter, this grant is awarded to one non-profit organization.

Business Specific Grants, $5,000
Every month, this grant is awarded to applicants whose businesses fall under a month-specific business category.

Mini Grants, up to $2,000
These are grants awarded to women owned businesses with smaller financial needs. The maximum for these mini grants is $2,000 per applicant with a $48,000 total to be awarded throughout the year.

More information for all of these grants can be found here.

NASE Growth Grants, up to $4,000
$4,000 are awarded monthly to support the growth of your small business. This money is meant to assit small businesses financially to help them grow in terms of employment, marketing, and more.

NASE Dependant Scholarship, up to $3,000
This scholarship is awarded to four applicants who will win $3,000 each to help cover the cost of highschool or college education for those ages 16-24 who are financially dependent on the applicant.

Learn more about these grants here.

FedEx Small Business Grant, $15,000 to $50,000
FedEx offers the FedEx Small Business Grant Contest Winners. There are many winners with prizes ranging from $15,000 to $50,000.

Learn more about this grant here.

WOSAB by the U.S. Small Business Administration
In an effort to "help provide a level playing field for women business owners," the government has provided contracts specificly for women owned small businesses. To be eligible for this program, a business must be at least 51% owned by a woman, be "small" by the SBA size standards, and the employees who make long term decisions as well as day to day tasks be women.

Learn more about this program here.
